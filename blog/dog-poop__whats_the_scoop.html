Dog Poop - What’s the scoop??

Hygiene and Health Implications Associated with the Incorrect Disposal of Dog Poop

If you own a dog, cleaning up dog poop will be part of your daily routine. This is a task we all hate doing, but it is very necessary. Dog poop can be harmful to your health and the incorrect disposal can cause many problems.

Using a Swoopet Pooper Scooper is your best choice as you will not have to handle the dog poop which is good for your health and your nose!

Dog Poop and the Law!

Firstly, it is illegal to not clean up after your dog. Local Authorities state that “Dog owners must remove their pets' waste from public places and dispose of it in a proper manner. This obligation applies to the following places:

  • Public roads and footpaths
  • Areas around shopping centres
  • School and sport grounds
  • Beaches
  • The immediate area surrounding another persons house

“Section 22 of the Litter Pollution Acts 1997 to 2009 requires that a person in charge of a dog shall immediately remove dog faeces from a public place or face an on-the-spot litter fine of €150. The maximum court fine is €3,000.” - Local Authority.

It’s a finable offence if you don’t clean up after your dog in a responsible manner.

Health Risks Associated with Dog Poop

Dog poop is an environmental hazard but also a health hazard. Dog poop can contaminate water supplies and cause all kinds of illness such as E.Coli and Coccidia.

Dog poop can also contain worms which can cause infection and disease in children and adults alike. It can cause an upset stomach, throat infections, asthma and even blindness. Cleaning up after your dog and regular deworming can eliminate these risks completely.

Dog poop can also carry and spread Parvo Virus - a very contagious and life threatening virus for dogs.

Children are particularly susceptible to being infected with germs and bacteria from dog poop. When they play in the garden or at the park or beach they can come in contact with dog poop without their parents ever knowing. Think about children before you walk away and fail to clean up!

When should I pick-up after my dog?

The simple answer here is ALWAYS! In particular you should alway clean up after your dog in the following places:

  • Public roads and footpaths
  • Parks and recreational areas
  • Areas around shopping centres & retail shops
  • Schools and sport grounds
  • Beaches
  • The immediate area surrounding another persons house

At home you should clean your own garden once a week to keep it dog poop free, clean and healthy.
